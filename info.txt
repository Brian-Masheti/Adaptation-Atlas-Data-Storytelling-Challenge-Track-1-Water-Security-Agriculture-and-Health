Track 1: Climate Impacts on Water Security, Agriculture and Health (in partnership with ATI)

This challenge, proposed by ATI, evaluates how climate change affects rainfall, evapotranspiration, soil moisture, and water availability across Africa. It supports decisions on irrigation, water conservation, and cross-sectoral allocation to reduce agricultural and household water stress under future scenarios.

Key questions to answer in Track 1:

    What regions experience the highest water stress?
    How does climate change impact water availability and distribution?
    What is the distribution of water use between agriculture, industry, and household consumption?
    What innovative technologies can help improve water efficiency and conservation?

See the Data tab for a more detailed description of the topic, the key questions, and suggested resources to answer each question.

Evaluation

This challenge will be evaluated in two phases - initial leaderboard position will be determined by preliminary rubric evaluation, and final winners will be decided based on expert panel evaluation of the top 10 Phase One scores in each track. Evaluation rubrics for Phase One and Phase Two are below.

The top ten participants in each track of Phase One will be asked to submit the URL link of their full notebook and supporting documentation for evaluation in Phase Two.

Phase one: Preliminary rubric evaluation

Your initial leaderboard score will be awarded, according to the following rubric:

    Format and layout (25%): Did you follow the given template?
    Graphs and other visualisations (25%): Did you include complete, accurate, and visually pleasing visualisations?
    Completeness (25%): Did you answer all of the critical questions for the topic?
    Narrative arc (25%): Did you tell a story?

Phase Two: Final rubric evaluation

If you achieve a score in the top 10 for a specific topic in Phase One, your notebook submission will be reviewed in Phase Two by an expert panel, according to the following rubric:

    Relevance (40%): How well were the questions addressed? Did the notebook achieve its goals overall?
    Usability, efficiency, and documentation (20%): How easy is it for stakeholders to use the notebooks in future?
    Creativity (20%): did you show creativity in your thought process and in how you told the story?
    Aesthetics and narrative (20%): were the graphs and visualisations successful? Did you tell a coherent story with your notebook?

Submission format

To make a submission in this challenge, users are required to fork and work directly from the Observable notebook template provided. You will export your Observable notebook to HTML and submit the HTML file for scoring on Zindi.

You will need to create a free Observable account to participate. Note that all Observable notebooks are either unlisted or public. We recommend that you keep your notebook unlisted for the duration of the challenge.

Exporting your Observable notebook to HTML with Notebook Kit

The Observable Notebook Kit module allows for an Observable notebook to be converted into a standalone HTML file for submission. To convert your Observable notebook into a static html, follow these steps:

    Have Node, Deno, Bun or a similar JavaScript runtime installed on your local device. We have tested the conversion with Bun 1.2, Deno 2.2 and Node.js 24.
    Install the Notebook Kit tool:

    npm add @observablehq/notebook-kit Or
    deno add npm:@observablehq/notebook-kit@latest Or
    bun install @observablehq/notebook-kit@latest

    Run the command line notebook-kit download command and write the output to a file.

Here is an example which shows how to convert the Observable notebook at https://observablehq.com/d/8a953dc1cde63a86 to a static html file called notebook.html
